  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose large containers with good drainage to prevent waterlogging.
    • Tuberose bulbs should be planted about 4-6 inches deep, so select containers with sufficient depth.
  2. Soil:
    • Use well-draining potting mix with a light texture.
    • Incorporate organic matter like compost to enhance soil fertility.
  3. Sunlight:
    • Tuberose plants prefer full sunlight, so position the containers in a sunny location.
    • They require at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily. /li>
  4. Bulb Planting:
    • Plant tuberose bulbs in spring or early summer.
    • Place bulbs about 6-8 inches apart in the container.
    • Plant them with the pointed end facing upwards.
  5. Seedlings or Seeds:
    • You can start with seedlings or sow seeds directly in the container, following the recommended spacing.
  6. Sunlight:
    • Position the containers in a location that receives full sunlight.
    • Eggplants require at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  7. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist during the growing season.
    • Water deeply when the top inch of soil feels dry.
    • Reduce watering after the flowering season to allow bulbs to dry.
  8. Pest and Disease Control:
    • Keep an eye out for pests like aphids or spider mites.
    • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil for natural pest control if needed.
  9. Fertilization:
    • Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer during the growing season.
    • Apply fertilizer every 2-3 weeks, following the package instructions.
  10. Fragrance:
    • Enjoy the sweet fragrance of tuberose flowers, especially in the evening. Planting them near seating areas can enhance the sensory experience.
