Onion

  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose containers that are at least 6-8 inches deep.
    • Onions have shallow roots, so you can plant them in relatively shallow containers.
  2. Soil:
    • Use well-draining potting mix with good fertility.
    • Onions prefer slightly acidic to neutral soil.
  3. Sunlight:
    • Position the containers in a location that receives full sunlight.
    • Onions prefer at least 6 hours of sunlight per day
  4. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during dry periods.
    • Water when the top inch of soil feels dry, but avoid overwatering, as onions don't like waterlogged soil.
  5. Onion Sets or Seeds:
    • Onions can be grown from sets (small bulbs) or seeds.
    • If using sets, plant them about 1-2 inches deep. If using seeds, follow the seed packet instructions.
  6. Spacing:
    • Space onion sets or seedlings at least 4-6 inches apart to allow room for bulb development.
  7. Mulching:
    • Apply a layer of mulch around the base of the onion plants to conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
  8. Pest and Disease Control:
    • Watch for pests like onion thrips and onion maggots.
    • Apply organic or chemical pesticides as needed and inspect plants regularly for signs of disease.
  9. Fertilization:
    • Onions benefit from a balanced fertilizer. Apply a general-purpose fertilizer according to package instructions.
  10. Harvesting:
    • Onions can be harvested when the tops begin to yellow and fall over. Once this happens, stop watering to allow the onions to cure.
    • After curing for a few days, you can gently lift the onions from the soil.
