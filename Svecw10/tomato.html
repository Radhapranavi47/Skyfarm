Tomato

  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose large containers with drainage holes to ensure proper drainage and prevent waterlogging.
    • Select containers that are at least 5 gallons in size to provide enough space for the tomato plant's roots.
  2. Soil:
    • Use a well-draining potting mix rich in organic matter.
    • Consider adding perlite or vermiculite to improve aeration.
  3. Sunlight:
    • Tomatoes thrive in full sunlight, so choose a sunny spot on your terrace.
    • Ensure the plants receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
  4. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
    • Water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch
    • Use a saucer under the container to catch excess water and prevent staining.
  5. Support:
    • Tomato plants may need support as they grow. Use stakes or cages to provide support and keep the plants upright.
  6. Fertilization:
    • Fertilize the plants regularly with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer according to package instructions.
  7. Pruning:
    • Remove suckers (side shoots) to encourage the main stem's growth and improve air circulation.
    • Prune as needed to control the size and shape of the plant.
  8. Pest and Disease Control:
    • Keep an eye out for pests such as aphids, whiteflies, and caterpillars
    • Use organic or chemical pesticides as necessary, and inspect plants regularly for signs of disease
  9. Mulching:
    • Apply a layer of mulch around the base of the plant to conserve moisture, regulate soil temperature, and suppress weeds.
  10. Harvesting:
    • Harvest tomatoes when they reach their mature color and are slightly soft to the touch.
