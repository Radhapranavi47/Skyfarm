Tulsi

  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose a large container with good drainage. Tulsi plants can be grown in pots or containers on your terrace.
  2. Soil::
    • Use well-draining potting mix with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.
    • Incorporate organic matter like compost for added fertility.
  3. Seedlings or Seeds:
    • You can start Tulsi from seeds or purchase seedlings from a nursery.
    • Sow seeds or transplant seedlings into the container, spacing them appropriately.
  4. Sunlight::
    • Tulsi prefers full sunlight. Position the container in a location that receives at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
  5. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
    • Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Tulsi doesn't like to dry out completely.
  6. Fertilization:
    • Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer or organic fertilizers like compost tea.
    • Fertilize every 2-4 weeks during the growing season
  7. Mulching:
    • Apply a layer of mulch around the base of the plants to conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
  8. Pruning:
    • Regularly prune mint to encourage bushier growth and prevent it from becoming leggy.
    • Pinch off the tips regularly to promote branching.
  9. Harvesting:
    • Harvest mint leaves regularly, especially before the plant flowers, for the best flavor.
    • You can harvest individual leaves or prune entire stems.
