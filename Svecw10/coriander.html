Coriander

  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose containers with good drainage to prevent waterlogging.
    • Coriander has a deep taproot, so use containers with sufficient depth.
  2. Soil:
    • Use well-draining, fertile soil with a neutral pH.
    • Incorporate organic matter, such as compost, to enhance soil fertility.
  3. Sunlight:
    • Coriander prefers partial to full sunlight.
    • Ensure the containers receive at least 4-6 hours of sunlight daily.
  4. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
    • Water when the top inch of soil feels dry.
    • Avoid overwatering, as coriander plants are susceptible to root rot
  5. Harvesting:
    • Harvest coriander leaves once the plant reaches a sufficient height (usually 6-8 inches).
    • Harvest the leaves regularly to encourage continuous growth.
  6. Pruning:
    • Pinch back the tips of the plants to encourage bushier growth.
    • Remove any yellow or damaged leaves regularly.
  7. Companion Planting:
    • Plant coriander near vegetables like tomatoes and peppers to enhance their flavor.
    • Avoid planting it near fennel, as they may cross-pollinate.
  8. Pest Control:
    • Keep an eye out for aphids, spider mites, and whiteflie
    • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil for pest control if necessary.
  9. Fertilization:
    • Use a balanced liquid fertilizer or a fertilizer specifically formulated for flowering plants..
    • Apply fertilizer every 4-6 weeks to support healthy growth.
  10. Successive Planting:
    • Successively sow coriander seeds every few weeks for a continuous harvest.
    • This helps ensure a fresh supply of leaves throughout the growing season.
