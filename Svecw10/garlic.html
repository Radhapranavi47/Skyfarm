Garlic

  1. Container Selection:
    • Choose containers with good drainage to prevent waterlogging.
    • Each garlic bulb should be planted individually, so select containers with sufficient space
  2. Soil:
    • Use well-draining, fertile soil with a neutral pH.
    • Garlic prefers a slightly acidic to neutral soil pH.
  3. Sunlight:
    • CGarlic prefers full sunlight for at least 6-8 hours a day.
    • Place containers in a location with ample sunlight exposure.
  4. Watering:
    • Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
    • Reduce watering as the garlic matures and the foliage begins to die back.
  5. Harvesting:
    • Harvest garlic when the lower leaves turn yellow and begin to dry out.
    • Lift the bulbs carefully, avoiding bruising or damage.
    • Allow the bulbs to cure in a dry, shaded area for a few weeks before storage
  6. Scapes Removal:
    • Garlic plants produce scapes (flower stalks). Removing them encourages the plant to focus energy on bulb development
    • Use the scapes in cooking for added flavor.
  7. Companion Planting:
    • Plant garlic near other crops as a natural pest deterrent.
    • It can help repel pests like aphids and certain fungi.
  8. Overwintering:
    • In cold climates, protect garlic containers from severe frost by moving them to a sheltered location or insulating them./li>
  9. Fertilization:
    • Side-dress with a balanced fertilizer when the garlic plants are actively growing.
    • Avoid excessive nitrogen as it can encourage more leafy growth at the expense of bulb development.
  10. Varieties:
    • Experiment with different garlic varieties to discover flavors and adaptability to your climate.
